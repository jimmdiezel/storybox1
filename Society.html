Introduction

Welcome to The Old Kent Society. TOKS is a family dedicated to analysing human development within Southwark and throughout humanity. We are establishing a platform designed to identify opportunities for personal growth and social development in our community before introducing our platform to the wider world. We intend to restrict deprivation and promote commercial investment in social cohesion by establishing consensus around our definition of the essential principles and values of a free society. We want to use these elements to analyse commercial activity and intend to create a universally approved classification for social development/ productivity. We believe that a structured framework of widely held principles and values will provide humanity with the freedom to build an alliance against abuse, deprivation and conflict.

TOKS started with the question ‘What is a healthy society?’. During our investigation we found that the question unintentionally drew the attention of those we asked towards concerns such as: smoking; cancer; cosmetic surgery; and mental health awareness. When we asked ‘What is a fair society?’ the responses would often relate to individual experiences and personal aspirations of ethics and justice. We started to ask ‘What is a free society?’ Freedom, like health and fairness, contained such a broad spectrum of beliefs that it was almost impossible to develop a definition that everyone could agree on.

Pillars of Humanity

At TOKS we believe the key to social development is solidarity. Unity and togetherness give the members of a society the confidence to pursue personal growth with the knowledge that they will be protected from exploitation and abuse. Institutions like the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) have published numerous reports documenting the value of social cohesion. Perspectives on Global Development (2012) described a cohesive society as one which “works towards the well-being of all its members, fights exclusion and marginalisation, creates a sense of belonging, promotes trust, and offers its members the opportunity of upward social mobility.” We used this definition to identify our essential elements of a free, fair and healthy society.

We identified 12 Pillars of Social Cohesion and believe that these principles and values are the essential elements of human development:

  • Merit
  • Mobility
  • Safety
  • Security
  • Ecology
  • Equality
  • Autonomy
  • Wellbeing
  • Integrity
  • Dignity
  • Family
  • Solidarity

Our vision of freedom is not restricted to any particular social group or political establishment. We want everyone to have the freedom to be part of a free, fair and healthy society and argue that a ‘fair’ or ‘healthy’ society is a cohesive society in which everybody has the opportunity to build on the value they inherently hold as human beings.

Human Trade Assessment

TOKS began to develop a questionnaire that gives workers the ability to anonymously identify the level of support they receive from their employment. The priority was to develop a standardized system that gives participants the opportunity to complete surveys in less than 7 minutes before comparing data from other organisations relevant to their profession, industry, location, demographic and employer. The Human Trade Assessment (HTA) is an ordinal survey with 12 questions that each correspond to a different aspect of the 12 Pillars of Social Cohesion. Each participants rates each element from 0 to 6, from: strongly disagree (0); through neutral (3); to strongly agree (6). Each dataset will take a measure of average across each element to produce a set of numerical figures for each participants to compare using visual aids such as graphs and charts.

  1. Does [your administration] support:
    1. Fair pay (decent wages)?
    2. Professional (career) development?
    3. Safety at work (including hygiene)?
    4. Job security (guaranteed hours)?
    5. Environmental (ecological) sustainability?
    6. Social equality (anti-discrimination)?
  2. Does [your administration] value:
    1. Freedom (Autonomy)?
    2. Health (Wellbeing)?
    3. Honesty (Integrity)?
    4. Respect (Dignity)?
    5. Community (Family)?
  3. Does [Your administration] cooperate with: industrial unions; independent workers associations; and collective bargaining?

We hope the HTA can gain public approval and have designed the survey to promote a comprehensive understanding of a free, fair and healthy society. Our assessment stands in contrast to economic evaluations that lack engagement; neglect personal experience; and measure economic development on the basis of finance and debt.

The HTA will be used build a platform that gives working people the opportunity to collectively evaluate the holistic value of economic activities.

Project Mayfair

TOKS intends to introduce the concept to the public by building a sample dataset that compares results from 7 secret locations within a short distance of the Old Kent Road. TOKS will begin distributing the HTA during project mayfair (PMF) and will collect completed paper assessments from 7 selected sites by hand or through the post.
